# Tax Deductions Guide for Self-Employed Professionals

## Home Office Deduction

If you use part of your home exclusively and regularly for business, you can deduct home office expenses.

### Requirements:
- Exclusive use: The space must be used ONLY for business
- Regular use: You must use it regularly for business activities
- Principal place of business: It must be your primary work location

### What You Can Deduct:
- Portion of rent or mortgage interest
- Property taxes
- Utilities (electricity, gas, water)
- Home insurance
- Repairs and maintenance
- Depreciation (if you own)

### Calculation Methods:
1. **Simplified Method**: $5 per square foot (max 300 sq ft = $1,500)
2. **Actual Expense Method**: Calculate actual percentage of home used for business

### Example:
- Home: 2,000 sq ft
- Office: 200 sq ft (10% of home)
- Annual expenses: $24,000
- Deduction: $2,400

## Business Equipment & Software

### Immediately Deductible (Section 179):
- Computers and peripherals
- Office furniture
- Software and subscriptions
- Equipment under $2,500

### Requirements:
- Used more than 50% for business
- Placed in service during tax year
- Keep purchase receipts

### Examples:
- MacBook Pro: $2,000 (100% deductible if 100% business use)
- Adobe Creative Cloud: $600/year
- External monitors: $500
- Ergonomic chair: $800

---

## Internet & Phone Expenses

### What's Deductible:
- Business percentage of internet service
- Business phone line
- Business cell phone usage

### How to Calculate:
Track business vs. personal usage for 1-2 months, then apply that percentage.

### Example:
- Internet: $80/month = $960/year
- Business use: 75%
- Deduction: $720/year

## Professional Development

### Deductible Education Expenses:
- Courses to maintain/improve job skills
- Professional certifications
- Books and publications
- Conference attendance

### NOT Deductible:
- Courses for a new career
- Undergraduate degree

### Examples:
- AWS Certification: $300
- Udemy courses: $400
- Technical books: $200
- Conference ticket: $500

## Health Insurance (Self-Employed)

If you're self-employed, you can deduct health insurance premiums for yourself, spouse, and dependents.

### Requirements:
- You cannot be eligible for employer-sponsored coverage
- Business must be profitable
- Can't exceed net profit from business

### Includes:
- Medical insurance
- Dental insurance
- Vision insurance
- Long-term care insurance (with limits)

## Business Travel

### Deductible Travel Expenses:
- Airfare
- Hotels
- 50% of meals during travel
- Car rental
- Uber/Lyft
- Tips

### Requirements:
- Travel must be primarily for business
- Keep detailed records
- Overnight stay required for full deduction

## Mileage Deduction

### 2024 Standard Mileage Rate: 67¢ per mile

### Requirements:
- Keep detailed mileage log
- Track business purpose
- Record odometer readings

### Example:
- Business miles: 5,000
- Deduction: 5,000 × $0.67 = $3,350

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Mixing personal and business expenses**
   - Keep separate accounts
   - Use separate credit cards

2. **Poor record keeping**
   - Save all receipts
   - Use accounting software
   - Document everything

3. **Claiming 100% when it's mixed use**
   - Be honest about percentages
   - Track actual usage

4. **Missing quarterly estimated tax payments**
   - Pay quarterly if you owe > $1,000
   - Avoid penalties

## When to Hire a Professional

Consider hiring a CPA or tax professional if:
- First year self-employed
- Income > $100,000
- Multiple income streams
- Complicated deductions
- Audit concerns
